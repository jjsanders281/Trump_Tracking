# Source Tier Policy

## Purpose
Define reliability tiers so verification decisions are consistent and auditable.

## Tier Definitions
- Tier 1: wire services, public media, court filings, official government records, full transcripts, full video.
  - Examples: AP, Reuters, PBS, court dockets, official agency releases.
- Tier 2: major outlets with standard editorial controls.
  - Examples: NYT, WSJ, WaPo, CNN, BBC.
- Tier 3: partisan/rage-bait or low-transparency publishers.
  - Not valid as sole evidence for verdict assignment.

## Minimum Evidence Rules
- Every claim requires one primary source (video, transcript, official post, or filing).
- Tier 1 primary + one corroboration can be sufficient for publication.
- If relying on Tier 2 reporting for a `false`, `misleading`, or `contradicted` verdict, require at least two independent corroborations.
- Tier 3 sources may be tracked as context but cannot determine verdicts.

## Disallowed as Verification Basis
- Outlets explicitly identified by project policy as unreliable for factual adjudication.
- Anonymous compilations with no attributable sourcing.
- Cropped clips where the full context cannot be reconstructed.

## Archiving
- Save archive links (Wayback, perma.cc, or equivalent) for every non-primary source.
