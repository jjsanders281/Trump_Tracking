# Shared Reference: Writing Standards

## Tone
- Use factual and neutral language.
- Avoid labels that imply intent unless evidence supports intent.
- Separate quote, claim extraction, and verdict rationale clearly.

## Rationale Rules
- Explain why the verdict applies in 2-5 direct sentences.
- Cite evidence type (transcript, filing, official record, independent reporting).
- State known uncertainty explicitly.

## Forbidden Patterns
- Emotional descriptors in verdict rationale.
- Ambiguous source references like "news reports say".
- Verdict assignment without evidence mapping.
